Marin County 2017-4-7

Our first stop in Marin County was the Muir Woods National Monument. If anyone isn't familiar with John Muir, well... you should be. I've heard told that the National Parks were "America's best idea", well, they were his idea. Thanks to my favorite president, Teddy Roosevelt, together they made it happen. This Redwood forest was named after him as was about a hundred other things dealing with nature in California.

Our second stop was Point Reyes National Seashore. A preserved chunk of wild coast with a picturesque lighthouse that gets nailed by a furious ocean. Wind and rain faded in and out as seals navigated the frigid waters some how not getting slammed to death into the rocks.
